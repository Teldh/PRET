Revision and tag labels description


DELETE
Background knowledge: the PR derives from the expert's domain knowledge since it's not expressed in the text;
Too far: the relation is too weak (i.e., in the path there are too many concepts in between the target and prerequisite concept);
Annotation error: mistake due to distraction;
Co-requisites: although related, there is no dependency relation between the two concepts (e.g. verbs to be and to have with respect to the concept of auxiliary verb);
Wrong direction: target and prerequisite concepts should be reversed in the pair.

CONFIRM
Lexical Relation: taxonomic relations, such as hypernyms (is-a relation: “a dog is a mammal”) or meronyms (part-of relation: “the wheel is a part of a car”).
Functional Relation: One of the two concepts uses the other or is needed by it (e.g., verbs use auxiliaries to express past time tense).
Definition: The prerequisite concept is used in the definition of the target concept (e.g., ”an integer is a number with no fractional component”).
Example: the target concept is explained as an example of another one (e.g., “ a polygon, such as a triangle”).
In Depth: The prerequisite concept is used as an in-depth description of another one (e.g., “Among auxiliaries, modal verbs etc.”).
Causal Relation: cause-effect relation between two concepts (e.g. “rain causes floods”)

WEIGHT
Weak: the prerequisite is useful but not strictly necessary
Strong: the prerequisite is absolutely necessary to understand the other concept
